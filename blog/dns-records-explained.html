What Are DNS Records? A, CNAME, MX, TXT & NS Explained for Beginners

Published: April 1, 2026 · 7 min read · OnlyFreeDomain Team

DNS records are the instructions that make the internet work — they tell browsers where to find your website, where to deliver your email, and more.

What Is DNS?

DNS stands for Domain Name System. It is essentially the phone book of the internet. When you type a domain name, DNS translates it into an IP address your browser can connect to.

The 5 Most Important DNS Record Types

A Record — Address Record

Points your domain name to an IPv4 address (like 76.76.21.21). This is the most fundamental DNS record. Change it when you move to a new hosting provider.

CNAME Record — Canonical Name Record

Creates an alias that points one domain name to another. Commonly used for www subdomains or third-party services like Shopify or HubSpot.

MX Record — Mail Exchange Record

Tells the internet which server handles email for your domain. Without MX records, nobody can send email to your domain.

TXT Record — Text Record

Stores text information about your domain. Most commonly used to verify domain ownership for Google Search Console, email authentication (SPF, DKIM), and security.

NS Record — Name Server Record

Specifies which DNS servers are authoritative for your domain. Change these when you switch from one hosting provider's DNS to another.

How Long Do DNS Changes Take?

DNS changes typically propagate within 15 minutes to 4 hours, though it can take up to 48 hours in rare cases. Lower your TTL to 300 seconds before making changes to speed this up.
